# TripSlip User Personas

## Experience Provider Personas

This section includes both traditional venue managers (museums, zoos, planetariums) and mobile/outreach education program directors (Junior Achievement, STEM programs, arts organizations) who provide educational experiences to schools.

### Persona 1: Sarah Chen - Museum Education Director

**Demographics:**
- **Age:** 38
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA (Mission District)
- **Role:** Director of Education at California Science Museum
- **Education:** Master's in Museum Studies from NYU, Bachelor's in Biology from UC Berkeley
- **Experience:** 12 years in museum education (5 years as director)
- **Salary:** $85,000/year
- **Family:** Married to Alex (software engineer), two kids (ages 5 and 8)
- **Tech Savviness:** High (9/10)

**Professional Background:**
Sarah started as a museum educator giving tours to school groups. She fell in love with seeing kids' faces light up during hands-on science demonstrations. After 7 years as lead educator, she was promoted to Director of Education. Now she manages a team of 8 educators and coordinates programs for 50,000+ students annually across 200+ Bay Area schools.

The museum serves diverse communities - from wealthy private schools in Pacific Heights to Title I schools in the Tenderloin. Sarah is passionate about making science accessible to all students, regardless of their school's budget. She's implemented scholarship programs and free admission days, but the administrative burden is crushing her.

**Personal Life:**
Sarah lives in a small apartment in the Mission with her husband Alex and their two kids. She bikes to work (30 minutes) and tries to maintain work-life balance but often checks email at 10pm after kids are asleep. Her own children attend public school, which gives her perspective on both sides of the field trip experience.

She's active in the museum community, attending conferences and networking with other education directors. She's known for being innovative and data-driven, often presenting at conferences about measuring educational impact.

A teacher from a Title I school emails asking about bringing her class but mentions they have no budget. Sarah wants to offer a scholarship but the process is complicated - she has to check available scholarship funds (in a different spreadsheet), get approval from her director (via email), send the teacher an application form (PDF), wait for it to be returned (often takes weeks), then manually process it. By the time it's approved, the teacher has often given up or the date is no longer available.

- **Education:** Associate's degree in Animal Science from Austin Community College

- **Salary:** $52,000/year
- **Family:** Divorced, two adult children (ages 24 and 27), lives alone
- **Tech Savviness:** Low-Medium (4/10)

**Professional Background:**

When the previous operations manager retired 8 years ago, the owner (a local family) promoted Marcus because of his loyalty and deep knowledge of the zoo.

Riverside Zoo is a small, beloved local attraction that serves about 15,000 visitors annually, including 3,000 students on field trips. It's not fancy - no corporate backing, no marketing department, just Marcus, 6 zookeepers, 2 education staff, and a part-time bookkeeper. The zoo survives on admission fees, memberships, and school bookings.

Marcus loves the animals and the mission, but he's overwhelmed by the business side. He never expected to be managing budgets, marketing, and technology. He's a hands-on guy who'd rather be working with animals than sitting at a computer.

**Personal Life:**
Marcus lives in a small house in East Austin that he bought 20 years ago (now worth 4x what he paid). He's been divorced for 10 years and his kids are grown and living in other cities. He's lonely sometimes but finds purpose in his work.

He wakes up at 5:30 AM every day to check on the animals before the zoo opens. He's at the zoo 6 days a week, often 10-12 hours a day. His social life revolves around the zoo - he's close with his staff and knows many regular visitors by name.

Marcus is skeptical of technology. He still uses a flip phone personally (though the zoo gave him a smartphone for work). He prefers face-to-face conversations and handwritten notes. He's worried about being left behind as the world goes digital, but he's also stubborn about learning new things.

She wants an automated system that suggests relevant add-ons based on the school's booking (e.g., "Schools like yours also added the Rocket Workshop"). She's seen this work in e-commerce and wants to bring that sophistication to education. But the current system is just a basic booking form with no intelligence.
