01 — Brand Foundation

Who We Are

TripSlip exists to make field trips work for everyone — venues, teachers, and parents. Our brand is warm, direct, and school-friendly. Built for classrooms, not boardrooms.

"We didn't build TripSlip from a boardroom. We built it from the driver's seat of a school bus, the coordination desk of a museum, and the classroom where field trips matter most."
— Achsha Jones, CEO & Founder · Former museum coordinator, CDL driver, lifelong Detroit educator
Energetic

Field trips are exciting — our brand matches that energy. Bold, confident, full of momentum. We move fast and make things happen for teachers and venues alike.

Trustworthy

We handle money, kids, and schedules. Every design decision signals reliability and transparency to every person in our network.

Practical

No fluff. We solve real problems — 17–30% revenue loss, 10+ wasted hours per week. Our product and brand say "this works," not "this looks cool."

Brand Voice

  • "Turning field trip headaches into high-fives."

04 — Logo & Mark

Logo System

The TripSlip logo combines a bold, stylized slip/receipt icon in TripSlip Yellow with a strong black wordmark. The jagged top edge of the mark references the classic tear-off permission slip — the product's origin story.

Primary — Yellow BackgroundDefault. Hero sections, marketing, print.
On WhiteApp headers, documents, emails.
Reversed — Dark BackgroundUse sparingly, only when yellow/white unavailable.
Mark Anatomy
The Slip Mark
Stylized permission slip / receipt with zigzag tear at top. Always in TripSlip Yellow (#F5C518) with dark shadow showing depth.
The Wordmark
Bold, condensed sans-serif. Black on light backgrounds, white on dark. Never alter the letterforms or spacing.
Clear Space
Minimum clear space = height of the slip mark icon on all four sides. Never crowd.
Logo Don'ts
Don't use low-contrast or washed out
Don't rotate or skew the logo
Don't stretch or distort
Don't use on off-brand backgrounds
Minimum Size: Never display the logo smaller than 120px wide in digital or 1.2" wide in print. Below this threshold, use the icon mark alone.

08 — Motion & Animation

How We Move

Motion should feel snappy and physical — like elements have weight. Bounce over fade. Snap over ease. The claymorphic icon style begs to be animated with playful bounces.

Bounce
Card hover, button press, icon appearances. The TripSlip signature feel.
Slide
Panel transitions, drawers, tab switches. Directional and purposeful.
Pulse
Loading states, pending payments, awaiting confirmations.
Spin
Processing only. Never decorative. Stop immediately on completion.
NameCurveDurationUse For
Snap (Brand)cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)150–250msButtons, cards, icon hover — the TripSlip bounce
Smoothcubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)200–350msPage transitions, drawer slides, modal opens
Entercubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.2, 1)250–400msElements entering — stagger 50ms per element
Exitcubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)150–200msElements leaving — always faster than entering
LinearlinearVariableSpinner rotations and continuous loops only
Motion Rules: Max 400ms (except page transitions). Every motion must communicate something — state change, progress, or feedback. On card hover: translate(-4px, -4px) + shadow expansion is the TripSlip card signature.
09 — Photography

Image Direction

Real people. Real field trips. Real joy. Photography should capture genuine excitement — students discovering the world, not staged corporate happiness.

Genuine.
Bright. Real.
✓ This is TripSlip

Students actively engaged — exploring, pointing, laughing, discovering. Natural light, warm tones, diverse groups. Captured moments, not posed ones. Venues look inviting and accessible.

Staged.
Dark. Generic.
✕ Not TripSlip

Stock photo smiles. Adults at whiteboards. Dark filters. Overly corporate settings. Homogeneous groups. Anything that feels like a studio shoot instead of an actual field trip.

✓
Natural, warm lighting — bright indoor venues or outdoor golden hour
✓
Genuine student reactions: wonder, excitement, curiosity, joy
✓
Recognizable Detroit / Michigan venues where possible
✓
Diverse student groups reflecting real classroom demographics
✓
Teachers engaged alongside students, not standing apart
✓
Color tones that complement yellow — warm, bright, never desaturated
✕
Staged or stock photos with forced, plastic smiles
✕
Dark, moody, or low-contrast photography
✕
Images of minors without proper model releases
✕
Heavy filters, extreme vignettes, or oversaturated edits
10 — Copy Library

Words That Work

Every headline, CTA, and error message carries the TripSlip voice — direct, warm, action-oriented. Keep it short. Make it real. Never corporate.

ContextCopy & Guidance
Hero — Venues
"Stop losing revenue to coordination chaos."
Leads with the pain. Immediate, specific. No jargon.
Hero — Teachers
"Stop burning hours on coordination chaos."
Same structure, different pain point — teachers feel seen immediately.
Sub-headline
"TripSlip connects venues, teachers, and parents in one system that recovers your money and saves everyone's time — guaranteed."
"Guaranteed" closes with confidence. Use intentionally.
Section Tagline
"Turning Field Trip Headaches Into High-fives."
Alliterative, true, and fun. Use as a consistent refrain.
CTA — Venues
"Join the TripSlip Platform" · "Recover My Revenue"
First-person CTAs ("My") convert higher. Use for key moments.
CTA — Teachers
"Create a TripSlip" · "Get My Time Back"
"Create a TripSlip" is the primary teacher action — always use as main CTA.
Empty State
"No trips yet — but that's about to change. Create your first TripSlip."
Never just "No data found." Empty states always invite action.
Success State
"High-five! Your trip is confirmed. 🎉 Detroit Children's Museum — March 15."
Celebrate the win. Include venue name and date for trust and specificity.
Error State
"Oops — that date's already booked. Pick another and you're good to go."
Friendly, blame-free, next step given immediately. Never "Invalid input."
Loading State
"Getting your venues ready..." · "Almost there..."
Conversational, not technical. Avoid "Processing request."
Urgency / Reminder
"3 students haven't paid. Your trip is in 4 days — send a nudge?"
Specific numbers create urgency. Always offer an immediate next action.
Onboarding
"Welcome — let's set up your first TripSlip in under 3 minutes."
Time promise reduces friction. "3 minutes" always beats "quickly."
