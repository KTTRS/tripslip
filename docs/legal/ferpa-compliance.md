# FERPA Compliance Documentation

**Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act Compliance**  
**Last Updated:** March 4, 2026

## Overview

TripSlip is fully compliant with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), 20 U.S.C. § 1232g. This document outlines our compliance measures and responsibilities.

## FERPA Requirements

### What is FERPA?

FERPA is a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. It applies to all schools that receive funds under an applicable program of the U.S. Department of Education.

### Key Provisions

1. **Parental Rights:** Parents have the right to inspect and review education records
2. **Consent Required:** Schools must obtain written consent before disclosing education records
3. **School Official Exception:** Schools may disclose records to school officials with legitimate educational interests
4. **Directory Information:** Schools may disclose directory information without consent if proper notice given

## TripSlip's Role

### School Official Status

TripSlip operates as a "school official" under FERPA's school official exception:

- We perform institutional services and functions
- We have legitimate educational interests in student data
- We use student data only for authorized educational purposes
- We maintain appropriate security measures

### Data Use Agreement

Schools using TripSlip must have a data use agreement that:
- Designates TripSlip as a school official
- Specifies permitted uses of student data
- Requires compliance with FERPA
- Prohibits unauthorized disclosure
- Requires data security measures

- **Authentication:** Multi-factor authentication available
- **Monitoring:** Continuous security monitoring and logging
- **Backups:** Regular encrypted backups
- **Incident Response:** Documented incident response procedures

### Administrative Safeguards

- **Training:** Staff trained on FERPA requirements
- **Policies:** Written data security and privacy policies
- **Audits:** Regular security and compliance audits
- **Vendor Management:** Third-party vendors contractually bound to protect data

### Physical Safeguards

- **Data Centers:** SOC 2 Type II certified facilities
- **Access Control:** Restricted physical access
- **Redundancy:** Geographic redundancy for disaster recovery

## Parental Rights

### Right to Inspect and Review

Parents have the right to:
- Access their child's education records
- Request copies of records
- Receive explanation of records
- Request amendment of inaccurate records

**How to Exercise:** Contact the school or email parents@tripslip.com

### Right to Consent

Parents must provide consent before:
- Student information is disclosed to unauthorized parties
- Student information is used for non-educational purposes
- Directory information is disclosed (if school requires consent)

## Data Disclosure

### Permitted Disclosures

We disclose student information only to:

1. **School Officials:** Authorized school personnel with legitimate educational interests
2. **Parents/Guardians:** Upon request or as necessary for service provision
3. **Service Providers:** Third parties under contract who perform services for us
4. **Legal Requirements:** When required by law or court order

### Disclosure Logging

We maintain audit logs of all disclosures including:
- Date and time of disclosure
- Parties to whom disclosed
- Purpose of disclosure
- Records disclosed
- Legitimate interest or consent basis

### Unauthorized Disclosure

We do not disclose student information:
- For marketing purposes
- To data brokers
- For non-educational purposes
- Without proper authorization

## Data Retention and Deletion

### Retention Period

- **Active Records:** Maintained while student is active in system
- **Historical Records:** Retained for 7 years after last activity
- **Audit Logs:** Retained for 7 years
- **Payment Records:** Retained for 7 years (tax requirements)

### Data Deletion

Upon request or after retention period:
- Student records are permanently deleted or anonymized
- Deletion completed within 30 days of request
- Confirmation provided to requesting party
- Audit logs maintained per legal requirements

### Right to Be Forgotten

Parents may request deletion of student data at any time by:
- Contacting the school
- Emailing privacy@tripslip.com
- Submitting written request

## Data Breach Response

### Notification Requirements

In the event of a data breach involving education records:

1. **Immediate Assessment:** Assess scope and impact within 24 hours
2. **School Notification:** Notify affected schools within 48 hours
3. **Parent Notification:** Schools notify parents without unreasonable delay
4. **Regulatory Notification:** Notify Department of Education if required
5. **Remediation:** Implement corrective measures immediately

### Breach Response Plan

Our incident response plan includes:
- Immediate containment and investigation
- Forensic analysis
- Notification procedures
- Remediation steps
- Post-incident review
- Documentation and reporting
